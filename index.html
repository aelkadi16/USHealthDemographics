Correlations Discovered Between Age, Income, Poverty Level Rate & Health Risks

Analysis 1: Percentage of population In Poverty
Overall, a general trend can be seen with the increase of poverty percentage within a US state, there is an increase in all health concerns. Additional observation reveals a trend of higher poverty percentages in Southeastern States, along with higher incidences of percentages of all three comparisons provided.

Analysis 2: Age (Median)
A look into the age comparisons gives insights into particular US States more-so than an overall trend. For example, WV has a higher average age, better healthcare, but unexpected higher percentages of obesity and smokers. Additionally, Northeastern States have a higher average age and better healthcare.

Analysis 3: Household Income (Median)
There is clearly a relationship between household income and the occurrence of obesity and smoking. The higher the income the less likely one is to smoke and have health risks. Analyzing household income against the lack of healthcare, there is a noticeable increase of risk with lesser incomes. Again, the southeastern states rank poorer than other states for healthcare, and the northeastern states are faring better. Two individual states are on the extremes; TX and MA would be worth looking into as to why TX has such a high percentage of the population lacking healthcare, and why MA is on the extreme of low in healthcare.
